What is the Saskatchewan CRS Calculator?

The Saskatchewan CRS Calculator estimates your Comprehensive Ranking System score for the SINP Express Entry Sub-Category — the pathway through which Saskatchewan nominates Express Entry candidates for Canada PR via the IRCC federal Express Entry pool.

Saskatchewan targets candidates from the Express Entry pool whose skills match provincial labour market needs. A successful SINP nomination adds 600 CRS points, virtually guaranteeing a federal Invitation to Apply (ITA). Check current CRS cutoff scores to see how your score compares to recent draw thresholds.

How is CRS calculated for Saskatchewan SINP?

The Saskatchewan PNP Express Entry Sub-Category operates within the federal Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS). Your score out of 1,200 covers core human capital (age, education, language, Canadian work experience), spouse factors, skill transferability, and additional points including nominations, job offers, siblings in Canada, and Canadian study credentials.

The single biggest CRS boost is the +600 SINP nomination bonus. Saskatchewan targets workers in high-demand occupations including agriculture, healthcare, trucking, and skilled trades. Review recent Express Entry draws and the CRS cutoff score history to understand current ITA thresholds.

Always cross-check criteria with the Government of Canada immigration portal before filing your Express Entry profile.

Frequently Asked Questions — Saskatchewan CRS Calculator
What is the Saskatchewan CRS calculator?

It estimates your CRS score for the SINP Express Entry Sub-Category to assess your chances of receiving a Saskatchewan provincial nomination for Canada PR through the federal Express Entry system.

What is a good CRS score for Saskatchewan?

A score above 450 is generally competitive for the SINP Express Entry pool. A SINP nomination adds 600 CRS points — making almost any base score competitive for a federal ITA. See the latest CRS cutoff scores for current draw context.

Does Saskatchewan use the federal CRS?

Yes. The SINP Express Entry Sub-Category operates within the federal CRS framework. Saskatchewan adds 600 CRS points when it nominates a candidate from the Express Entry pool.

What occupations does Saskatchewan target through SINP?

Saskatchewan prioritises workers in healthcare, agriculture, trucking and transportation, skilled trades, and technology occupations. The province targets candidates whose skills align with its current labour market needs.

How can I improve my Saskatchewan CRS score?

Raise your IELTS score to CLB 9+, gain Canadian work experience, complete a Canadian credential, or secure an LMIA-backed Saskatchewan job offer. See the full CRS improvement guide.
