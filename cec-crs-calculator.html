      What is the Canadian Experience Class (CEC)?

      The Canadian Experience Class (CEC) is one of three main programs under Canada's Express Entry immigration system. It is designed for people who already have skilled work experience inside Canada and want to obtain permanent residence.

      Unlike the Federal Skilled Worker (FSW) program, CEC applicants do not need to meet the 67-point FSW selection factor threshold. Instead, they compete in the Express Entry pool based entirely on their CRS score and wait to receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA) in a draw.

      CEC vs FSW – Key Differences

      Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

      • Requires ≥1 yr Canadian work experience
      • No 67-point FSW threshold needed
      • Higher CRS points for Canadian work exp
      • Work must be in past 3 years
      • Ideal for people already working in Canada

      Federal Skilled Worker (FSW)

      • Requires ≥1 yr skilled foreign work experience
      • Must meet 67/100 selection factor points
      • Foreign work exp counts toward CRS
      • No requirement to be in Canada
      • Ideal for overseas applicants

      CEC Eligibility Requirements 2026

      To qualify for the Canadian Experience Class, you must meet all of the following requirements set by IRCC:

      • 1 year of skilled Canadian work experience — must be in a NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 occupation (skilled jobs) within the past 3 years before applying.
      • Minimum language scores — CLB 7 for NOC TEER 0 or 1 jobs; CLB 5 for NOC TEER 2 or 3 jobs. Must be from an approved test (IELTS, CELPIP, TEF, or TCF).
      • Plan to live outside Quebec — Quebec has its own immigration system. CEC is for applicants planning to settle in other Canadian provinces.
      • Not a Canadian citizen or PR — CEC is only for foreign nationals seeking to become Canadian permanent residents.

      How to Improve Your CEC CRS Score in 2026

      • Improve IELTS/CELPIP scores: Moving from CLB 7 to CLB 9 in all four abilities can add 30–50 CRS points. Language is the fastest way to increase your score without waiting.
      • Gain more Canadian work experience: Each additional year of Canadian work experience adds points both directly and through Skill Transferability bonuses. Aim for 3+ years.
      • Apply to a provincial PNP stream: Many provinces have Express Entry-aligned streams that target CEC candidates. A provincial nomination adds +600 CRS points. See our PNP CRS Calculator.
      • Secure a valid job offer: An LMIA-supported job offer adds +50 points (most NOC levels) or +200 points (senior management).
      • Add French language skills: French CLB 7+ adds +15 to +30 bonus points and may qualify you for lower-cutoff French-language category draws.
      • Add a Canadian sibling: If you have a sibling who is a Canadian PR or citizen and living in Canada, claim +15 additional points.

      CEC CRS Calculator – Frequently Asked Questions

      What is the Canadian Experience Class (CEC)?
      The Canadian Experience Class (CEC) is an Express Entry immigration stream for workers who already have at least 1 year of skilled paid work experience in Canada within the past 3 years. CEC applicants are scored using the CRS formula and compete for ITAs in IRCC's regular draws without needing to meet the 67-point FSW threshold.
      How is the CRS score calculated for CEC applicants?
      CEC applicants use the same full CRS formula — age (up to 110 pts), education (up to 150 pts), language (up to 136 pts), Canadian work experience (up to 80 pts), skill transferability (up to 100 pts), and additional factors like PNP (+600 pts) or job offer (+50/200 pts). Canadian work experience is a key differentiator — 5+ years adds 80 points versus 25 for 3+ years of foreign experience.
      What is the minimum CRS score for CEC in 2026?
      There is no fixed minimum CRS score for CEC. In 2026, all-program draw cutoffs have ranged from 470 to 530. When IRCC runs dedicated CEC draws (as it did in 2022–2023), the cutoffs may be lower. A CEC score of 450+ is generally considered competitive, especially when combined with a PNP nomination.
      Does Canadian work experience carry more weight in CEC than FSW?
      Yes. In the CRS formula, 1 year of Canadian work experience is worth 40 points (single applicant), while 1–2 years of foreign experience is worth only 13 points. Furthermore, Canadian work experience combines with language and education scores to generate Skill Transferability bonuses worth up to 50 additional points — making CEC applicants typically higher-scoring than equivalent FSW applicants.
      Can I apply to CEC while on a work permit in Canada?
      Yes. Most CEC applicants are currently in Canada on a valid work permit (open or employer-specific). You can create an Express Entry profile and enter the pool while still working on your permit. You must have your language test results ready and have completed at least 1 year of skilled Canadian work experience before submitting your profile.
      What NOC jobs qualify for CEC?
      CEC requires experience in a NOC TEER 0 (management), TEER 1 (professional), TEER 2 (technical), or TEER 3 (trades/technical support) occupation. NOC TEER 4 and TEER 5 jobs (service and labour) do not qualify. You can look up your job's NOC code at Canada.ca/NOC.
