What is the Canadian Experience Class (CEC)?

The Canadian Experience Class (CEC) is one of three federal immigration programs managed through Canada Express Entry. It is designed for skilled workers who are already living and working in Canada.

Unlike the Federal Skilled Worker program, CEC does not require a job offer or Canadian education. Your existing Canadian work experience is the foundation of your eligibility.

CEC is one of the most competitive Express Entry programs. In 2025–2026, IRCC held multiple CEC-specific draws per month, often with the highest CRS cutoffs among all draw types.

✅

CEC draws are currently dominant in Express Entry. If you have 12+ months of skilled Canadian work experience, CEC is your primary pathway to permanent residence.

How to Apply for CEC — Step by Step

1

Take Language Test

Book IELTS, CELPIP (English) or TEF/TCF (French). Aim for CLB 9+ to maximize CRS points.

2

Gather Documents

Employment letters, pay stubs, T4 slips, reference letters confirming NOC TEER and hours worked.

3

Create Express Entry Profile

Enter your details on the IRCC Express Entry portal. Profiles are valid for 12 months.

4

Wait for ITA

IRCC draws from the pool every 1–2 weeks. If your CRS score exceeds the cutoff, you receive an ITA.

5

Submit PR Application

You have 60 days after receiving an ITA to submit your complete permanent residence application.

6

Receive PR Decision

IRCC's standard processing time for Express Entry applications is 6 months for 80% of cases.
