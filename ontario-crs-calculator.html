What is the Ontario PNP (OINP)?

The Ontario Immigrant Nominee Program (OINP) is Ontario's provincial immigration program. It allows the province to nominate skilled workers, international students, and entrepreneurs for Canadian permanent residence.

For Express Entry candidates, the OINP's enhanced streams are the most powerful pathway. An OINP nomination adds 600 CRS points instantly — virtually guaranteeing an ITA at the next federal draw.

Ontario is Canada's most populous province and economic hub. It is home to Toronto, Ottawa, and the Greater Golden Horseshoe — making it the top destination for skilled immigrants.

🏛️

Key fact: You cannot apply to OINP directly. Ontario searches the Express Entry pool and sends a Notification of Interest (NOI) to eligible candidates. You must then apply within 45 days.

How the OINP Express Entry Process Works

The OINP Human Capital Priorities Stream is invitation-only. Here's the step-by-step process:

1

Create Express Entry Profile

Enter the federal Express Entry pool under FSW or CEC. Use our free CRS calculator first to estimate your score.

2

Receive NOI from Ontario

OINP searches the pool and sends a Notification of Interest (NOI) to eligible candidates. You cannot request an NOI — wait for Ontario to select you.

3

Apply to OINP Within 45 Days

Once you receive your NOI, you have exactly 45 calendar days to submit your OINP application. Missing this deadline voids the NOI.

4

Receive Ontario Nomination

If approved, Ontario sends you a Nomination Approval Letter via your Express Entry account. You have 30 days to accept the nomination.

5

Get +600 CRS Points & ITA

Accepting the nomination instantly adds 600 CRS points to your profile. You will receive an ITA at the next Express Entry draw.

6

Apply for Canadian PR

After receiving your ITA, submit your PR application to IRCC. Standard processing is 6 months. You must intend to live in Ontario.

Working & Settling in Ontario

Ontario is Canada's most economically diverse province. It offers immigration-friendly cities across multiple industries.

Top Ontario Cities for Immigrants

  • Toronto: Canada's financial capital. Home to Bay Street, tech corridors, and over 200 languages spoken. Top NOC sectors: finance, IT, healthcare.
  • Ottawa: Federal government hub. Strong demand for public servants, IT professionals, and bilingual (English/French) workers.
  • Mississauga & Brampton: Part of the Greater Toronto Area. Strong manufacturing, logistics, and healthcare sectors. Lower cost of living than Toronto.
  • Hamilton: Growing tech and creative economy. Affordable housing. Close to Toronto via GO Transit.
  • Kitchener-Waterloo: Canada's "Silicon Valley." Home to Shopify (HQ), Google Canada, and hundreds of tech startups.
  • London, Ontario: Strong healthcare, education, and insurance sectors. Affordable housing and a large university student population.
✅

Having ties to Ontario — such as a job offer, family, previous work, or study experience in Ontario — significantly strengthens your OINP application and reduces the risk of rejection.

The official minimum CRS score for the OINP Human Capital Priorities Stream is 400 points. However, Ontario selects candidates at its discretion. In recent rounds, candidates with CRS scores of 450–491 were more likely to receive an NOI. A lower CRS score can still lead to an NOI if you have strong Ontario ties (job offer, past Ontario education/work experience).
An Ontario PNP nomination adds +600 CRS points instantly. This means that even a candidate with a CRS score of 420 would have 1,020 points after nomination — far above any draw cutoff. Ontario nomination essentially guarantees an ITA at the very next Express Entry draw. Use our full CRS calculator to see your score with and without PNP points.
Requirements include: (1) Active Express Entry profile under FSW or CEC; (2) Minimum CRS score of 400; (3) CLB 7 or higher in all four language skills; (4) Bachelor's degree or higher (ECA required for foreign credentials); (5) At least 1 year of skilled work experience in NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3; (6) Intent to live and work in Ontario; (7) Sufficient settlement funds. FSW applicants also need 67+ on the federal 6-factor grid.
An OINP Notification of Interest (NOI) is an invitation from the Ontario government to apply for provincial nomination. It is issued through your Express Entry account. Once you receive an NOI, you have exactly 45 calendar days to submit your full OINP application online. Applications submitted after 45 days will not be processed, and you will need to wait for a new NOI.

However, having an Ontario job offer strengthens your application and is mandatory for the Employer Job Offer streams. A valid Ontario job offer also adds 50–200 points to your federal CRS score — check our Express Entry calculator for details.
The OINP French-Speaking Skilled Worker Stream is for Express Entry candidates who speak French at CLB 7 or higher and English at CLB 6 or higher. This stream typically has lower CRS cutoffs than the Human Capital Priorities Stream. It requires an active Express Entry profile, at least 1 year of work experience in NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3, and the intention to live and work in Ontario.
OINP application processing typically takes 60 to 150 days after submission for most streams. Once nominated, you accept the nomination in your Express Entry profile (within 30 days) and receive +600 CRS points. You then typically receive an ITA within 1–2 Express Entry draws.
