What is Canada Express Entry?

Canada Express Entry is the federal government's online system for managing applications for permanent residence from skilled workers. Managed by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC), it uses the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) to rank candidates in a pool and invite the highest-scoring profiles to apply for Canadian permanent residency.

Express Entry covers three federal immigration programs and a pathway via provincial nominations. Candidates in the pool are ranked by CRS score. IRCC conducts regular draws — typically every two weeks — and issues Invitations to Apply (ITAs) to candidates above the draw cutoff.

Express Entry Eligibility Requirements

Before calculating your Express Entry CRS score, you must first meet the minimum eligibility requirements for at least one of the three programs. Here is a summary:

Federal Skilled Worker (FSW) Minimum Requirements

  • At least 1 year of continuous skilled work experience (NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3) in the past 10 years
  • Minimum language scores of CLB 7 in all four skills
  • Canadian educational credential, OR a foreign credential assessment (ECA)
  • Minimum 67/100 points on the FSW 6-factor grid

Canadian Experience Class (CEC) Minimum Requirements

  • At least 1 year of skilled Canadian work experience in the past 3 years
  • Minimum language scores of CLB 7 (NOC TEER 0 or 1) or CLB 5 (NOC TEER 2 or 3)
  • Plan to live outside Quebec (Quebec has its own immigration system)

Federal Skilled Trades (FST) Minimum Requirements

  • At least 2 years of skilled trade work experience (NOC TEER 2 or 3) in the past 5 years
  • Minimum language scores of CLB 5 (speaking/listening) and CLB 4 (reading/writing)
  • Valid job offer for at least 1 year OR a certificate of qualification in a trade
⚠️

Meeting eligibility requirements does not guarantee an ITA — your CRS score must also exceed the draw cutoff. See the CRS improvement guide if your score is below recent cutoffs.

Express Entry CRS Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

Canada Express Entry is IRCC's online immigration management system for selecting skilled workers for permanent residence. The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) ranks all candidates in the Express Entry pool based on human capital factors (age, education, language, work experience), spouse factors, skill transferability, and additional factors like provincial nomination or job offers. IRCC draws from this pool and invites the highest-scoring candidates to apply for PR.
The CRS cutoff varies per draw. Recent all-program draw cutoffs have ranged from 470 to 525 points. The most recent all-program draw (#320) had a cutoff of 489. French-language draws have had cutoffs as low as 371. Category-based draws (STEM, healthcare, trades) have their own cutoffs. Track historical cutoffs on our CRS Cutoff Scores page.
The three federal Express Entry programs are: (1) Federal Skilled Worker (FSW) — for international skilled workers with 1+ year of foreign experience; (2) Canadian Experience Class (CEC) — for workers with 1+ year of Canadian work experience; (3) Federal Skilled Trades (FST) — for tradespeople with 2+ years of experience in an eligible trade. All three use the same CRS score formula.
Once you receive an ITA and submit a complete PR application, IRCC's service standard is 6 months for 80% of applications. This processing time starts from when IRCC receives your complete application, not from when you entered the pool. Applications requiring additional documentation or security checks may take longer. Entering the pool and waiting for an ITA is a separate step that depends entirely on your CRS score and draw cutoffs.
The maximum possible CRS score for Canada Express Entry is 1,200 points. This includes: up to 500 points for core human capital factors (single applicant), up to 40 points for spouse/partner factors, up to 100 points for skill transferability, and up to 600 points for additional factors (provincial nomination alone adds 600 points).
