What is the Alberta CRS Calculator? (AAIP Express Entry 2026)

Quick Answer: The Alberta CRS Calculator estimates your federal Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score and checks it against Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP) Express Entry stream thresholds for 2026.

The Alberta CRS score calculator uses the official IRCC Comprehensive Ranking System criteria to estimate your points across four sections: Core Human Capital (age, education, language), Skill Transferability, Canadian work experience, and Additional Points.

Alberta's AAIP then uses its own internal score (out of 100) to rank and invite Express Entry candidates. The minimum AAIP score required varies by stream — typically 58–63 for Tech, 54–61 for Priority Sectors, and a base federal CRS of 300 for the general Alberta Express Entry stream.

If you receive an AAIP provincial nomination, IRCC automatically adds +600 federal CRS points to your profile — virtually guaranteeing an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for Canada PR.

How is CRS Score Calculated for Alberta PNP? Full Breakdown

The federal CRS score used for Alberta Express Entry is calculated from the same IRCC CRS grid as all other Express Entry streams. Maximum is 1,200 points (without PNP) across four core sections:

  • Section A – Core Human Capital (500 pts): Age (110 max), Education (150 max), Language (160 max), Canadian work experience (80 max)
  • Section B – Spouse/Partner Factors (40 pts): Spouse education, language, and Canadian work experience
  • Section C – Skill Transferability (100 pts): Education + language combinations, work experience combinations
  • Section D – Additional Points (600 pts): PNP nomination (+600), job offer (+50 or +200), Canadian education (+15/+30), French language (+25/+50), sibling in Canada (+15)
Alberta Tip: Working in Alberta's priority sectors (healthcare, tech, construction, trades) significantly increases your chances of receiving a Notification of Interest (NOI) from AAIP, even at lower CRS scores.
