What is the Alberta AAIP (formerly AINP)?

The Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP) — formerly known as the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP) — is Alberta's provincial immigration program.

It allows Alberta to nominate skilled workers, tech professionals, healthcare workers, and entrepreneurs for Canadian permanent residence. For Express Entry candidates, the AAIP Express Entry Stream is the fastest route.

Alberta's nomination adds +600 CRS points to your federal Express Entry profile, virtually guaranteeing an ITA at the next draw.

⭐

Alberta advantage: With a minimum CRS of only 300 points, the AAIP Express Entry Stream is the most accessible PNP in Canada — much lower than Ontario (400) or BC (minimum varies by stream).

How the Alberta AAIP Express Entry Process Works

The AAIP Express Entry Stream is invitation-only. You cannot apply directly — Alberta selects candidates from the federal pool.

1

Create Federal Express Entry Profile

Enter the federal Express Entry pool under FSW, CEC, or FST. Ensure your CRS score is at least 300 and your occupation supports Alberta's economy. Use our free CRS calculator.

2

Indicate Interest in Alberta

In your Express Entry profile, express interest in living in Alberta. Alberta searches the pool for eligible candidates matching its priority sectors.

3

Receive a Notification of Interest (NOI)

Alberta issues an NOI to selected candidates via their Express Entry profile. You cannot request an NOI — you must be selected by AAIP.

4

Apply to AAIP via Portal

Once you receive an NOI, submit your full AAIP application through the Alberta immigration portal within the specified deadline. Provide supporting documents.

5

Receive Alberta Nomination (+600 CRS)

If approved, Alberta nominates you and your federal CRS score jumps by +600 points. You accept the nomination in your Express Entry account within 30 days.

6

Receive ITA & Apply for Canadian PR

With 600 extra points, you receive an Invitation to Apply at the next Express Entry draw. Submit your federal PR application. IRCC processes within 6 months.

Working & Settling in Alberta, Canada

Alberta is Canada's most economically dynamic western province. It has no provincial income tax on the first $21,003 of earnings and offers strong wages across its key industries.

Top Alberta Cities for Immigrants

  • Calgary: Alberta's largest city. Canada's energy capital. Thriving tech, finance, and construction sectors. Home to multiple Fortune 500 energy companies.
  • Edmonton: Provincial capital. Strong healthcare, government, and education sectors. Home to the University of Alberta — a top-ranked research institution.
  • Red Deer: Central Alberta hub. Manufacturing, agriculture, and healthcare. Lower cost of living than Calgary or Edmonton.
  • Lethbridge: Southern Alberta agricultural and education hub. University of Lethbridge. Strong agri-food sector.
  • Fort McMurray: Resource sector hub. Oil sands capital of Canada. High wages. Key destination under Rural Renewal and Opportunity streams.
  • Grande Prairie: Northwest Alberta. Growing healthcare, construction, and oil & gas workforce. In-demand trades workers.

Alberta's Priority Sectors in 2026

Alberta is actively recruiting workers in these sectors through the AAIP:

  • 🏥 Healthcare: Physicians, nurses, allied health professionals, personal support workers
  • 💻 Technology: Software developers, data analysts, cybersecurity experts, IT managers
  • 🔧 Construction & Trades: Electricians, plumbers, carpenters, welders, heavy equipment operators
  • 🌾 Agriculture: Farm managers, agri-food processing, agricultural equipment technicians
  • 🏭 Manufacturing: Industrial mechanics, machinists, quality control technicians
  • 🚔 Law Enforcement: Police officers, correction officers, border services officers
🏔️

Alberta has no provincial sales tax (PST) — the only province other than the Northwest Territories without one. This significantly increases take-home pay for Alberta immigrants compared to other provinces.

AAIP vs AINP — Are They the Same?

Yes — AAIP and AINP are the same program. In 2022, the Alberta government rebranded the Alberta Immigrant Nominee Program (AINP) as the Alberta Advantage Immigration Program (AAIP). The streams, eligibility criteria, and nomination process remain the same.

If you find older resources referring to AINP — including "AINP CRS calculator" or "Alberta AINP Express Entry" — they are all referring to what is now called the AAIP.

The Alberta Opportunity Stream is a base (non-Express Entry) stream for workers already living and working in Alberta. Requirements include: a valid work permit, at least 12 months of Alberta work experience in the last 18 months (or 24 months Canadian experience in 30 months), a valid job offer from an Alberta employer, minimum CLB 4 or 5, and a high school diploma. This stream is for workers inside Alberta — it is not linked to the federal Express Entry pool. Visit the official AAIP streams page for full details.
