What is the Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)?

The Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) allows Canadian provinces and territories to nominate immigrants who meet their specific labour market or community needs. Once nominated, a candidate receives +600 CRS points in the Express Entry pool — virtually guaranteeing an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for permanent residence.

There are two types of PNP streams: Enhanced streams (Express Entry-aligned, add +600 CRS points, fast-tracked) and Base streams (paper-based, slower processing, no CRS impact). This calculator focuses on Enhanced streams.

🏛️

In 2025–2026, IRCC held PNP-specific Express Entry draws approximately every 2–4 weeks. Even candidates with a base CRS score of 100–200 can receive an ITA after getting nominated, since 200 + 600 = 800+ exceeds almost all PNP draw cutoffs.

How the PNP Express Entry Process Works

1

Create Express Entry Profile

Enter the federal Express Entry pool by creating a profile on the IRCC portal. You must qualify for FSW, CEC, or FST.

2

Receive NOI or Apply Directly

Provinces search the Express Entry pool and send Notifications of Interest (NOIs). Some provinces also accept direct applications from candidates with job offers.

3

Apply to Provincial Stream

Submit your provincial PNP application. Processing takes weeks to months depending on province and stream.

4

Receive Provincial Nomination

The province approves your application and issues a nomination certificate. You have 60 days to accept in Express Entry.

5

+600 CRS Points Added

Accepting the nomination adds 600 CRS points to your profile instantly. Your new score virtually guarantees you an ITA in the next PNP draw.

6

Receive ITA & Apply for PR

IRCC issues ITAs in PNP-specific draws. You then submit your complete PR application within 60 days.

PNP CRS Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

A valid provincial nomination through an Express Entry-aligned (Enhanced) PNP stream adds exactly 600 CRS points to your profile. This is a flat bonus — it applies the same regardless of which province nominates you, your occupation, or your base score. It is the largest single CRS bonus available and virtually guarantees an ITA in the next PNP-specific draw.
PNP draw cutoffs appear high because every candidate in the PNP pool has already received +600 points. A candidate with a base CRS of 200 will show 800 in the pool. So when the cutoff is 802, it means the lowest-ranked candidate had a base score of about 202. The practical barrier for PNP candidates is getting the provincial nomination, not the CRS score after nomination. See our CRS cutoff scores history for all draw results.
There is no single "easiest" province, as requirements change frequently based on labour market needs. Generally: Saskatchewan SINP is known for lower CRS thresholds (sometimes as low as 60–70 points in their provincial pool). Alberta AINP frequently draws candidates with lower scores. Nova Scotia NSNP has targeted specific occupations with lower thresholds. Manitoba MPNP is accessible for those with connections to Manitoba. The best strategy is to check current provincial draws and align your occupation with provinces actively targeting your field.
Enhanced PNP (Express Entry-aligned): Requires an active Express Entry profile. After nomination, adds +600 CRS points. IRCC processing target: 6 months. Candidates enter the Express Entry pool and receive an ITA through PNP-specific draws.

Base PNP (Paper-based): Does not require Express Entry profile. Goes through a separate paper-based process outside Express Entry. No CRS impact. Processing time: 18–24 months. Both types lead to Canadian permanent residence, but Enhanced is significantly faster.
It depends on the stream. Many PNP streams require a job offer (e.g., OINP Employer Job Offer, BC PNP Skills Immigration with employer support, NB with employer). However, several streams do not require a job offer: Ontario Human Capital Priorities (IRCC selects based on CRS), Alberta Express Entry Stream, Saskatchewan Express Entry (for some occupations), and Manitoba Skilled Workers Overseas. Streams without job offer requirements often have higher minimum CRS score thresholds at the provincial level.
Yes. You can apply to multiple provincial PNP streams simultaneously, as long as you meet each province's eligibility requirements. However, once you receive a provincial nomination and accept it in your Express Entry profile, you are indicating intent to live in that province. You can only have one active nomination in your Express Entry profile at a time. There is no federal rule against applying to multiple provinces, but some provincial streams may have residency or employment intent requirements.
