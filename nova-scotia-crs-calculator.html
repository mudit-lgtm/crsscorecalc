What is the Nova Scotia CRS Calculator?

The Nova Scotia CRS Calculator estimates your Comprehensive Ranking System score for the NSNP Express Entry Stream — the pathway through which Nova Scotia nominates federal Express Entry candidates for Canada PR via the IRCC Express Entry pool.

Nova Scotia issues Notifications of Interest (NOIs) to candidates in the federal pool who meet provincial criteria. A successful NSNP nomination adds 600 CRS points, virtually guaranteeing an Invitation to Apply (ITA). Check the latest CRS cutoff scores to benchmark your score against recent draws.

How is CRS calculated for Nova Scotia NSNP?

Nova Scotia's Express Entry stream operates within the federal Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS). Your score out of 1,200 covers core human capital (age, education, language, Canadian work experience), spouse factors, skill transferability, and additional points including nominations, job offers, siblings in Canada, and Canadian study.

The strongest single boost in any provincial CRS calculation is the +600 NSNP nomination bonus. Even a baseline score of 400 becomes highly competitive once Nova Scotia issues a nomination. Review recent Express Entry draw results and the CRS cutoff history to understand what score has led to ITAs.

For official criteria, always cross-check with the Government of Canada immigration portal before filing your Express Entry profile.

Frequently Asked Questions — Nova Scotia CRS Calculator
What is the Nova Scotia CRS calculator used for?

It estimates your CRS score for the NSNP Express Entry Stream so you can assess your chances of receiving a Nova Scotia provincial nomination for Canada PR through the federal Express Entry system.

What is a good CRS score for Nova Scotia?

A score above 450 is generally competitive for the NSNP Express Entry pool. With a nomination, Nova Scotia adds 600 CRS points — making even mid-range scores highly competitive for a federal ITA. See the latest CRS cutoff scores.

Does Nova Scotia use the federal CRS points system?

Yes. The NSNP Express Entry Stream runs inside the federal pool and uses the standard federal CRS framework. Nova Scotia adds 600 CRS bonus points when it nominates a candidate.

How can I improve my Nova Scotia CRS score?

The most impactful improvements are: raise your IELTS score to CLB 9+, gain Canadian work experience, complete a Canadian credential, or obtain an LMIA-backed job offer in Nova Scotia. Read the full CRS improvement guide.
