What is the BC PNP (British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program)?

The British Columbia Provincial Nominee Program (BC PNP) allows the province of British Columbia to nominate skilled workers and international graduates for Canadian permanent residence.

BC PNP is one of the most active provincial nominee programs in Canada. It holds regular draws across multiple occupational categories — Tech, Healthcare, Construction, Childcare, and more.

When BC nominates you, you receive +600 CRS points added to your federal Express Entry profile, making an Invitation to Apply (ITA) virtually guaranteed in the next federal draw.

⭐

Key advantage: Even a CRS score of 400 becomes 1,000+ with a BC PNP nomination — well above any historical all-program draw cutoff.

BC PNP runs five main Skills Immigration streams, each with its own eligibility criteria and minimum score thresholds. All streams offer an Express Entry BC (EEBC) option.

BC PNP Calculator — Frequently Asked Questions

BC PNP Skills Immigration minimum scores vary by draw category in 2026: Tech stream: 120, Healthcare: 99–100, Construction: 89–93, Childcare: 87–90, and Veterinary Care: 80. The overall BC PNP score grid has a maximum of 200 points. Your BC PNP score and your federal CRS score are separate — the BC PNP score determines if BC will invite you, while your CRS score determines your federal Express Entry ranking. See all draw results on WelcomeBC.ca.
The Express Entry BC (EEBC) stream links your BC PNP application directly to your federal Express Entry profile. To access EEBC, you must be registered in the Express Entry pool (eligible for CEC, FSW, or FST) AND meet BC PNP stream eligibility requirements. When BC nominates you through EEBC, IRCC automatically adds +600 CRS points to your profile, virtually guaranteeing an ITA in the next federal draw regardless of your base score.
A British Columbia PNP nomination adds exactly +600 CRS points to your federal Express Entry profile. Since the typical all-program draw cutoff is around 500–527, even a base CRS of 400 becomes 1,000+ with a BC nomination — well above any historical cutoff. This makes BC PNP the single most powerful pathway for candidates with CRS scores below 500.
The BC PNP Tech Pilot is a priority processing stream for workers in 29 eligible technology NOC codes (software engineers, developers, data analysts, IT managers, etc.). It offers 2–3 week provincial nomination processing. To qualify, you need: a full-time BC job offer in an eligible tech NOC, at least 1 year of directly related work experience, minimum CLB 4 language, and a minimum BC PNP score of 120. You must also be eligible for BC PNP's Skilled Worker or International Graduate stream.
Most BC PNP Skills Immigration streams require a full-time job offer from a BC employer. However, the International Post-Graduate stream (for master's and PhD graduates from BC universities) does not require a job offer. For all other streams — Skilled Worker, International Graduate, Tech Pilot, Healthcare, Construction — a valid, full-time job offer from an eligible BC employer in a skilled occupation is mandatory.
The BC PNP Skills Immigration grid assigns up to 200 points across two categories. Economic Factors (max 80 pts): Hourly wage of your BC job offer (max 55 pts) + Regional district of employment (max 25 pts — rural areas score higher). Human Capital Factors (max 120 pts): Directly related work experience (max 40 pts) + Education (max 40 pts) + Language proficiency (max 40 pts). In each draw, candidates with the highest scores in their stream category receive invitations to apply.
