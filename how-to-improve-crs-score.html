Why Your CRS Score Matters

The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score determines your position in Canada's Express Entry pool. IRCC conducts regular draws and invites the highest-scoring candidates to apply for permanent residence. The latest all-program cutoff was 489 points (Draw #320, March 2025).

If your current CRS score falls below the cutoff, you need to actively work on improving it. This guide walks through all 11 proven strategies to boost your CRS points — from quick wins to long-term investments — with exact point values for each improvement based on the official IRCC CRS criteria.

💡

First step: Calculate your current CRS score so you know your baseline and exactly which sections are holding you back.

1

Improve Your Language Test Score (IELTS / CELPIP)

Potential gain: +10 to +128 CRS points | Difficulty: Medium | Timeline: 1–6 months
⬆ Up to +128 pts

Language scores are the single most impactful factor you can improve without a job offer or nomination. The CRS assigns points for each of the four language skills (Speaking, Listening, Reading, Writing) for both your first and second official language. Even improving by one CLB level across all four skills can add 28–50 CRS points.

2

Apply for a Provincial Nomination (PNP)

Potential gain: +600 CRS points | Difficulty: Medium–High | Timeline: 3–12 months
⬆ +600 pts

A Provincial Nominee Program (PNP) nomination is the single biggest CRS booster available. When you receive a provincial nomination, IRCC adds 600 points to your CRS score automatically — virtually guaranteeing an ITA in the very next draw.

Each Canadian province and territory runs its own PNP with specific streams targeting workers in occupations where they have labour shortages. You can simultaneously be in the Express Entry pool and apply to a PNP stream.

3

Secure a Valid Canadian Job Offer

Potential gain: +50 to +200 CRS points | Difficulty: Hard | Timeline: Varies
⬆ Up to +200 pts

A valid Canadian job offer from an eligible employer can add 50 to 200 CRS points depending on the NOC TEER category:

  • +200 points: NOC TEER 0, Major Group 00 (senior management: CEO, CFO, COO level)
  • +50 points: Any other NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 occupation

Note that most job offers require a valid Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) or must be exempt from LMIA requirements (e.g., intra-company transfers, Canada–US–Mexico Agreement workers, or specific work permit holders).

⚠️

Important: Not all Canadian job offers qualify for CRS points. The offer must be for a permanent, full-time position and meet IRCC's specific requirements. Verify your eligibility with the IRCC job offer guidelines.

4

Gain Canadian Work Experience

Potential gain: +40 to +80 CRS points per year | Difficulty: Medium | Timeline: 1+ years
⬆ +40–80 pts/year

Canadian work experience is one of the highest-value CRS factors. Even one year of skilled Canadian work experience adds 40 points for a single applicant. This also qualifies you for the Canadian Experience Class (CEC), which typically has lower draw cutoffs than FSW.

5

Improve Your French Language Score

Potential gain: +25 to +50 bonus points + lower-cutoff French draws | Difficulty: Hard | Timeline: 6–18 months
⬆ +25–50 pts bonus

Improving your French to CLB 7 or higher unlocks a direct French-language bonus on top of your regular language points:

  • +25 points if your French is CLB 7+ and your English is CLB 4 or lower
  • +50 points if your French is CLB 7+ and your English is CLB 5 or higher

Even more importantly, IRCC regularly holds French-language-specific draws with cutoffs as low as 371 points — significantly lower than all-program draws. If your base CRS is in the 400–450 range, achieving French CLB 7 via the TEF Canada or TCF Canada exam could be your fastest path to an ITA.

6

Get a Higher Level of Education

Potential gain: +14 to +28 CRS points | Difficulty: Hard | Timeline: 1–4 years
⬆ Up to +28 pts

Education level directly affects your core human capital CRS score. Upgrading from a Bachelor's degree to a Master's adds 14 points; from a Master's to a PhD adds another 14 points. While this is a long-term strategy, completing a Master's degree in Canada also qualifies you for the Canadian education bonus (see Strategy 7 below).

7

Complete Post-Secondary Education in Canada

Potential gain: +15 to +30 CRS points | Difficulty: Medium | Timeline: 1–3 years
⬆ +15–30 pts

Completing an eligible post-secondary program at a Canadian institution adds bonus points under the Additional Factors section:

  • +15 points: 1 or 2-year post-secondary program completed in Canada
  • +30 points: Post-secondary program of 3 years or more completed in Canada

This strategy combines well with Strategy 4 (Canadian work experience) and Strategy 6 (higher education). Completing a 2-year college diploma in Canada while working part-time can simultaneously boost multiple CRS categories. Learn more about studying in Canada on IRCC.ca.

8

Add a Second Official Language Score

Potential gain: +4 to +24 CRS points | Difficulty: Medium | Timeline: 6–12 months
⬆ Up to +24 pts

Many applicants only submit scores for their first official language (English or French) and miss free CRS points by not submitting a second language test. If your first language is English, submitting a French test (TEF or TCF) can add up to 24 CRS points for your second language skills alone — completely separate from the French bonus in Strategy 5.

  • CLB 5 in any second language skill = 1 point per skill (max 4 points)
  • CLB 6+ in any second language skill = 1 point per skill (max 4 points)

Use our IELTS to CRS calculator or CRS score calculator to see the exact impact of adding a second language score to your profile.

9

Leverage Spouse / Partner CRS Factors

Potential gain: +10 to +40 CRS points | Difficulty: Medium | Timeline: Varies
⬆ Up to +40 pts

If your spouse or common-law partner is accompanying you to Canada, their education, language scores, and Canadian work experience all contribute CRS points. Improving your spouse's profile can meaningfully increase your combined score:

  • Spouse education: Up to +10 points (PhD level)
  • Spouse first official language: Up to +20 points (CLB 9+, all 4 skills)
  • Spouse Canadian work experience: Up to +10 points (5+ years)
ℹ️

If your spouse has significantly lower scores, consider whether applying as a single applicant (with the other as a dependent only) produces a higher total CRS. Use our CRS calculator to compare both scenarios.

10

Gain Foreign Work Experience (Skill Transferability)

Potential gain: +13 to +50 CRS points | Difficulty: Long-term | Timeline: 1–3 years
⬆ Up to +50 pts

Foreign work experience contributes to your CRS score through the Skill Transferability section when combined with strong language scores or Canadian work experience. The grid rewards combinations, not individual factors:

  • 1–2 years foreign experience + CLB 7+ first language = +13 points
  • 3+ years foreign experience + CLB 7+ first language = +25 points
  • 1–2 years foreign experience + CLB 9+ = +25 points (bonus stacks)
  • Foreign experience + Bachelor's degree or higher = additional +13–25 points

Skill transferability points cap at 100 points total across all combinations. Calculate your current skill transferability points with our CRS score calculator.

11

Time Your Entry — Target Lower-Cutoff Draws

Potential gain: Strategic advantage | Difficulty: Low | Timeline: Monitoring only
🎯 Strategic

Not all Express Entry draws have the same cutoff score. Category-based selection draws introduced in 2023 invite candidates in specific occupations with significantly lower cutoffs:

  • French-language draws: Cutoffs of 350–400 (vs 470–525 for all-program)
  • STEM occupation draws: Cutoffs of 481–496
  • Healthcare occupation draws: Often 430–470 range
  • Trade occupations draws: Historically lower cutoffs

Monitor CRS cutoff score history and IRCC's announced draw categories. If your NOC falls under a priority category, you may be invited at a lower score than the all-program cutoff. Check the official IRCC Express Entry Rounds of Invitations to track draw patterns.

⚠️

Don't just wait passively. Express Entry profiles expire after 12 months. Use the waiting period productively to implement the strategies above and increase your score.
