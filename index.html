What is CRS Score?

The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) is a points-based system used by Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada (IRCC) to rank candidates in the Express Entry pool. Your CRS score is based on factors like age, education, language ability, and work experience. The higher your score, the better your chances of receiving an Invitation to Apply (ITA) for Canadian permanent residence.

Scores range from 0 to 1,200 points. IRCC conducts regular Express Entry draws and invites the highest-scoring candidates to apply for permanent residence. Recent all-program draw cutoffs have ranged from approximately 470 to 525 points.

The CRS score determines where your profile ranks in the Express Entry pool relative to other candidates. Using this free CRS calculator, you can estimate your score and identify areas for improvement before submitting your official profile. For a full explanation, see our What is CRS Score guide.

How is CRS Score Calculated?

The CRS score is calculated using a four-section formula based on the official IRCC CRS criteria. The maximum possible score is 1,200 points. Here's how each section contributes:

  • Section A — Core Human Capital (max 500 pts): Age, level of education, first official language, and Canadian work experience
  • Section B — Spouse/Partner Factors (max 40 pts): Spouse's education, language scores, and Canadian work experience (only if spouse is accompanying you)
  • Section C — Skill Transferability (max 100 pts): Combinations of foreign work experience + language, education + language, or trade certification
  • Section D — Additional Points (max 600 pts): Provincial nomination (+600), job offer (+200 or +50), French language bonus, Canadian education, sibling in Canada

For a complete points breakdown with tables, visit our How CRS Score is Calculated guide.
